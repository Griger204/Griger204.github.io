Khan Academy - AP®︎/College Computer Science Principles

This course includes 800+ practice questions, plus instructional articles and videos covering the topics of digital information, the Internet, cybersecurity, programming, algorithms, simulations, and data analysis. These combine for a total of 4 modules.

Digital Information
This unit explains how computers store information digitally, as binary data. Teaches about the binary number system, the conversion of analog to digital data, and data compression strategies.

The Internet
This unit covers how the Internet works. Teaches about the physical layer (Ethernet, Fiber, WiFi), the protocols (IP, TCP, UDP, DNS, HTTP), the World Wide Web, and the digital divide.

Programming
This unit introduces fundamental programming concepts. Teaches about variables, strings, procedures, Boolean logic, randomness, repetition, and lists, with examples in JavaScript, Snap, Python.

Algorithms
This unit gives an overview of algorithms: expressing algorithms in flow chart and pseudocode, testing their correctness, and measuring their efficiency. I also explored heuristics-based approaches for problems that can’t be solved in a reasonable amount of time, introduce the existence of undecidable problems, and explain the computational methods of parallel and distributed computing.

Data analysis
The unit introduces data analysis and the world of big data. Teaches about storing data sets in files, spreadsheets, and databases, computing statistics like average and maximum, finding patterns like trends and correlations. Learn how big data can be used to improve algorithms like translation, image recognition, and recommendations.

Simulations
This unit explores simulations, with interactive examples from various fields. Teaches how to use random numbers and walk-through the process of creating a simple simulation.

Online Date Security
This unit introduces the concept of PII and the ways in which PII and other data can be tracked online (cookies, search history, browsing history, geolocation). Teaches how attackers can gain access to data and devices with phishing, rogue access points, and malware, and see how you can protect yourself with encryption, secure protocols, strong passwords, and multifactor authentication.

Computing innovations
This unit explores a wide range of computing innovations that are possible now due to computers and the Internet, and considers the benefits and risks of each innovation. Teaches about social media, citizen science, smart sensors, and more.

This course has tought me that there is so much more to computer science than just code. Computer science is a whole stream of connections between the code and how that code is gathered, processed, distributed and used. Computer's and the internet all around the world work in a system that allows information to flow through it safely, efficiently, and correctly. There are so many dependant variables on how information flows through us over digitally that it's important to understand how these stuff work so we could only make it better. All these topics here impact our day-today lives, so I will continue to further understand these topics and algorithms for better sense of what happens when I press "run" or share my code with others or even the world.
